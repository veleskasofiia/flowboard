FlowBoard — How It Works

End-to-end architecture of your app

Entry point 🧑‍💻
You (the user)
Open FlowBoard in your browser at composio-dashboard-mu.vercel.app
↓
sign in / sign up
Authentication 🔐
Supabase Auth
Email & password login. Creates your account and gives you a unique User ID — this ID is your key to all your data and connected apps.

Also stores: messages (AI chat history) · workflows (saved canvas)
↓
User ID passed everywhere
Page 1 📊
Dashboard
Your weekly overview. Calls /api/summary to fetch:
• Meetings this week
• Unread Gmail (inbox)
• Unread Outlook mail

Cached 5 min in localStorage.
→
Page 2 🔗
Connect Apps
Calls /api/connect → Composio starts OAuth flow → you log in to Google / Microsoft → tokens stored in Composio under your User ID.

Apps: Gmail · Google Calendar · Outlook · Slack · Notion · Drive · Discord · Todoist
→
Page 3 ⚡
Workflow Builder
Drag & drop nodes onto a canvas. Connect them with arrows. Save to Supabase. Press Run to execute.

Canvas saved to localStorage + Supabase workflows table.
↓
▶ Run button pressed
The Bridge 🌉
Composio
Holds your OAuth tokens. Translates tool calls into real API requests to third-party services using your account. Every call goes through your User ID so Composio knows which account to use.

tool results returned
AI — Workflow Runner 🦙
Groq · LLaMA 70B
Powers the ▶ Run button. Gets your nodes, calls Composio tools in a loop (up to 10 steps), then writes a plain-English summary of what happened in your accounts.
·
AI — Chat Assistant 🤖
Claude (Anthropic)
Powers the AI Flow Assistant sidebar. Can answer questions, help you build workflows, and call Composio tools directly to read your real data. Chat history saved to Supabase.

result shown to you
